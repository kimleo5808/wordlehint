The New York Times now runs three flagship daily word games — Wordle, Connections, and Strands — and a small industry of "hints and answers" pages has grown up around each one. Most of them are the same: a wall of ads, the answer dumped at the top so you get spoiled before you've read a sentence, and a publish time that lags the puzzle by hours.

A genuinely good hint site does the opposite. This is a short, opinionated guide to the ones worth bookmarking and, more importantly, **what to look for** so you can judge any site yourself.

## What makes a hint site worth using

Four things separate a useful daily-puzzle helper from a spoiler trap:

1. **Spoiler-safe by default.** You should never see the answer until you choose to. The best sites reveal help in stages — a vague clue first, then a stronger one, then the solution — so you can take exactly as much as you need.
2. **Accurate and on time.** A new puzzle drops at midnight local time. If the site hasn't updated, or shows yesterday's answer, it's useless. Daily, automated updates matter.
3. **Depth, not just the word.** A definition for the answer, the puzzle number, the editor, letter data — these turn a quick lookup into something you actually learn from.
4. **Clean and fast.** If you have to scroll past three ad units to reach the hint, close the tab.

Judge any site against those four and you'll quickly sort the keepers from the rest.

## Wordle

Wordle is the simplest of the three, but the help around it varies wildly. What you actually want is a **progressive hint** — a clue you reveal one step at a time — rather than the answer shoved in your face.

- For the daily clue, a staged [Wordle hint for today](/wordle-hint-today) lets you take a nudge instead of the full word and keep your streak honest.
- If you only want to confirm the solution, a [past Wordle answers archive](/wordle-answers) with definitions is more useful than a bare list — you can check whether a word has been used and learn what it means.
- Before you even open the puzzle, your opener matters more than anything. A data-backed look at the [best Wordle starting words](/best-wordle-starting-words) — ranked by how they perform against real answers — will sharpen your first guess.
- And when you're a few letters in and stuck, a [Wordle solver](/wordle-solver) that filters by your green, yellow, and gray tiles narrows the field in seconds.

For a second, independent opinion, the NYT's own [WordleBot](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2025/upshot/wordlebot.html) analyzes your game after you finish — it's the most authoritative Wordle tool there is, though it's behind the NYT Games subscription.

## Connections

Connections is where a good hint site really earns its keep, because the puzzle is built to mislead you. Many words look like they fit several groups, and the trap is usually the most obvious-looking one.

The best approach is layered help: a clue for each color group first, then the category names, then the four words — never all at once. Our own [Connections hint for today](/connections-hint-today) follows exactly that staged structure, and the [Connections archive](/connections-answers) lets you search any past answer by word, category, or puzzle number.

If you want a site built entirely around the game, [ConnectionsHint](https://connectionshint.app/) is a solid dedicated option that covers the daily puzzle in depth. A single-game focus can be nice when Connections is the only puzzle you care about.

One habit that helps more than any hint: when you think you've found a group, scan the other twelve words before you submit. Connections almost always plants a decoy.

## Strands

Strands is the newest of the three and the hardest to find good help for. It's a themed word search with a twist — the **spangram**, a single word that stretches across the whole board and names the theme.

A good Strands helper gives you the theme clue, then a nudge toward the spangram (its first letter and length), then each theme word individually — so you can solve most of it yourself. Our [Strands hint for today](/strands-hint-today) works that way, and the [Strands archive](/strands-answers) keeps every recent spangram and theme-word set searchable.

For a site focused only on Strands, [StrandsHint](https://strandshint.app/) tracks each day's spangram and theme words. As with Connections, a dedicated single-game site is worth a bookmark if Strands is your main puzzle.

The best Strands strategy is to find the spangram early. It spans the entire grid, so it's long and usually anchors a corner — and once you have it, the theme is confirmed and the shorter words fall into place.

## Honorable mentions

If you want a more editorial take, the big tech-media puzzle desks — Tom's Guide and TechRadar among them — publish daily hint articles for all three games. They're reliable and well written, though they lean toward longer prose and more ads than a purpose-built hint tool.

## The bottom line

The single best habit isn't choosing the perfect site — it's **using the least help you can**. Take the vague clue before the strong one, the category name before the words, the first letter before the spangram. A good hint site is one that makes taking less help easy.

Bookmark one trustworthy helper per game, keep the daily puzzles in a row, and you'll get the satisfaction of solving without the frustration of being truly stuck.
